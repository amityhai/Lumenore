Privacy Notice

Lumenore is committed to protecting your privacy and compliance with all relevant legislation, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), where this applies to EU citizens, and CCPA where this applies to Californian citizens.

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how we collect, use, store, process, and share your information in relation to this website. This Policy covers our treatment of information that is gathered by this website, including personally identifiable information or personal data. This Policy does not apply to information collected by third-party websites, services, or personal data collected in the context of employment.

Questions or concerns? Reading this privacy notice will help you understand your privacy rights and choices. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our Services. If you still have any questions or concerns, please contact us at support@lumenore.com.

Summary of Key Points

What personal information do we process? When you visit, use, or navigate our Services, we may process personal information depending on how you interact with Lumenore and the Services, the choices you make, and the products and features you use.

Do we process any sensitive personal information? We do not process sensitive personal information.

Do you receive any information from third parties? We do not receive any information from third parties.

How do you process my information? We process your information to provide, improve, and administer our Services, communicate with you, for security and fraud prevention, and comply with the law. We may also process your information for other purposes with your consent. We process your information only when we have a valid legal reason to do so.

In what situations and with which parties do we share personal information? We may share information in specific situations and with specific third parties.

How do we keep your information safe? We have organizational and technical processes and procedures in place to protect your personal information. However, no electronic transmission over the internet or information storage technology can be guaranteed to be 100% secure.

What are your rights? Depending on where you are located geographically, the applicable privacy law may mean you have certain rights regarding your personal information.

How do I exercise my rights? The easiest way to exercise your rights is by filling out our data subject request form available at lumenore.com/contact, or by contacting us. We will consider and act upon any request in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

Security and Governance for Generative AI in Lumenore

Lumenore's Association with Microsoft Azure's Open AI

It is important to note that Lumenore uses models from Microsoft Azure, which has a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with Open AI. This agreement guarantees the same strong data privacy standards for these models. Additionally, Lumenore doesn't share any user personal information data with OpenAI by any means and respects user preferences and privacy.

Data Privacy Commitment

The Business Associate Agreement guarantees the same robust data privacy for Lumenore AI models as Azure, ensuring that business data shared for AI insights is protected and never reused.

Metadata-Only Processing

All Lumenore services — including Root Cause Analysis (RCA), Data science analytics, visualization processing, agent-based workflows, and natural language querying — only process metadata. No raw customer data is shared with external parties for these services.

Generative AI Insights – Secure Data Handling

The only scenario where user's business data leaves the Lumenore environment is when AI insights are generated using models integrated with Microsoft Azure's OpenAI. Data is shared securely and processed over encrypted channels:

  • Users are prompted to provide additional consent before securely sharing their business data for AI insight processing. This consent can be withdrawn at any time when handling sensitive data and can be re-enabled for non-sensitive data directly from the same interface.
  • A clear callout informs users: "Business data shared for generating AI insights is protected by a secure AI layer with no use for training purposes."

User-Specific Policy Enforcement

Based on the Policy Engine Flow, each user has different policy classifications. These classifications — based on the type of content, data type, and usage — are checked by the Firewall Policy Engine:

  • If the content is compliant with the allowed policy, it is allowed and processed normally.
  • If the content violates the user's policies, it is blocked, and the associated policy is enforced. Each action is taken according to the user's predefined policies, ensuring compliance with organizational data governance frameworks.

No Data Sharing for Other Services

All other product capabilities and analytics do not require any data to leave the secure Lumenore environment. No user business data is shared externally under these conditions.

Lumenore's BYOK Model

Lumenore allows users to bring their own key for all types of AI generated transactions/affairs. This means users manage their API keys, maintaining full control over their data in the OpenAI integration.

Lumenore LLM

Lumenore uses LLM models securely hosted within Azure environments. Data is not shared with public OpenAI endpoints for any purpose.

User Controls and Compliance

  • Transparency and Consent — Users are notified when business data sharing may occur for AI insights and given the option to review and accept the terms for data usage.
  • Compliance — Lumenore meets all required privacy and security regulations, taking advantage of Microsoft Azure's certifications and compliance features.

Do We Use Cookies and Other Tracking Technologies?

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are used to store small pieces of information. They are stored on your device when the website is loaded on your browser. These cookies help us make the website function properly, make it more secure, provide a better user experience, understand how the website performs, and analyze what works and where it needs improvement. For further information, visit allaboutcookies.org.

How do we use cookies?

Like most online services, our website uses first-party and third-party cookies for several purposes. First-party cookies are mostly necessary for the website to function the right way, and they do not collect any of your personally identifiable data. The third-party cookies used on our website are mainly for understanding how the website performs, how you interact with our website, keeping our services secure, providing relevant advertisements, and providing a better user experience.

Duration

  • Session cookies — Temporary; expire once you close your browser (or once your session ends).
  • Persistent cookies — Remain on your hard drive until you erase them or your browser does, depending on the cookie's expiration date. According to the ePrivacy Directive, they should not last longer than 12 months.

Provenance

  • First-party cookies — Put on your device directly by the website you are visiting.
  • Third-party cookies — Placed on your device by a third party like an advertiser or analytics system.

Purpose

  • Strictly necessary cookies — Essential for you to browse the website and use its features, such as accessing secure areas of the site.
  • Preferences cookies — Allow a website to remember choices you have made in the past, like language or login preferences.
  • Statistics cookies — Collect information about how you use a website. All data is aggregated and anonymised.
  • Marketing cookies — Track your online activity to help advertisers deliver more relevant advertising. These are persistent and almost always third-party cookies.

Manage Cookie Preferences

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking the Cookie Settings button on the Lumenore homepage. You can also manage cookies directly through your browser:

  • Chrome
  • Safari
  • Firefox
  • Internet Explorer

Controls for Do-Not-Track Features

As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that we must follow in the future, we will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this privacy notice.
