Service Levels

7.1 Scope of Technical Support

Technical Support is intended to provide assistance to Named Customer Contacts for incidents and product issues beyond what is covered in the Documentation. Technical Support services consist of web-based submissions of incidents, providing workarounds or solutions for product issues, access to Supported Channels, and troubleshooting.

Severity 1 Issues

Critical incidents with severe performance impact or features not operating as documented, impacting 90% of the users or product functionality.

Severity 2 Issues

Incidents with performance impact or features not operating as documented, impacting at least 40% of the users or product functionality.

Severity 3 Issues

How-to questions and product issues with performance impact or features not operating as documented, impacting a small minority of users.

7.1.2 What Technical Support Excludes

  • Unavailability caused by circumstances beyond our reasonable control, including acts of God, acts of government, emergencies, natural disasters, flood, fire, civil unrest, acts of terror, strikes or other labor problems, or any other force majeure event.
  • Problems resulting from Client's combining or merging the Subscription Service with any hardware or software not supplied by us or not identified by us in writing as compatible with the Subscription Service.
  • Interruptions or delays resulting from telecommunications or internet service provider failures outside of our datacenter as measured by our third-party website availability monitoring provider.
  • Any interruption or unavailability resulting from the misuse, improper use, alteration, or damage of the Subscription Service.
  • Operational Issues.

7.2 Incident Severity and Response Times

The time for response and resolution is determined by the agreed terms and conditions outlined in the contract or statement of work.

7.3 Service Uptime

For the purposes of this section:

"Downtime" is unplanned outage/severe issue that constitutes a catastrophic problem causing complete inability to use the Subscription Service, excluding Free Services, across a significant portion of the production environment (e.g. crash or hang), resulting in production downtime and where there is no workaround or solution to the problem. This excludes planned outages communicated in advance through subscribed channels.

"Service Uptime" is calculated using the following formula:

Service Uptime = (total hours in calendar month − Excluded duration − Downtime duration)
                        ÷ (total hours in calendar month − Excluded duration) × 100%

We will use reasonable efforts to meet a Service Uptime of 99.9% for our analytical service in each calendar month. All availability calculations will be based on our system records. This 'Service Uptime' section does not apply to our Free Services.

7.4 Policy Terms

Coverage Hours and Business Hours are determined by the agreed terms and conditions outlined in the contract or statement of work.

Refund and Cancellation

8.1 Cancellations at Convenience and Refunds

You can cancel your Lumenore subscription at any time. However, canceling will turn off the auto-renewal of your subscription. Your Lumenore subscription automatically renews for periods equal to your initial purchase. For example, if you have chosen a one-month subscription, the subscription will automatically renew for another 30 days at the end of the first month if you do not cancel within the first 30 days.

Once you cancel, you will not be charged again, and your benefits will continue until the end of the billing cycle. However, you won't be refunded for the period between your cancellation and the end of your subscription.

8.2 Cancellations for Cause and Refunds

The licenses may be canceled upon a breach or performance failure on the part of Lumenore. The Lumenore Customer Service Desk needs to be notified of such a breach within a reasonable time frame from when the issue is known to you. If the issue is not resolved within 15 (fifteen) days of such notice, you may cancel the contract and may be eligible for a refund for the period such default persists.

For the sake of clarity, performance failure is meant to define only critical failures, such as the application not completely functioning, the application being broken, or complete disruption of service.
